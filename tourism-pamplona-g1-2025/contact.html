Frequently Asked Questions

To get to Pamplona, Negros Oriental from Dumaguete City, you can ride a Ceres bus going to Tanjay, which takes about 30 minutes. From Tanjay, ride a pot-pot (local tricycle) up to Pamplona in about 10–15 minutes. The whole trip takes roughly 40–45 minutes. If you’re using a private vehicle or taxi, you can drive directly from Dumaguete to Pamplona in about 30–40 minutes

The best time to visit Pamplona, Negros Oriental is from January to February for the coolest and driest weather, or April to May for the sunniest and driest conditions.

Yes, Pamplona in Negros Oriental is generally considered safe for tourists, especially after law enforcement efforts have led to a drop in the crime rate in the province.

A must-try local delicacy near Pamplona, Negros Oriental is Budbud Kabog, a unique suman made from millet instead of rice. It’s steamed in banana leaves and has a soft, slightly nutty flavor that many locals enjoy for breakfast or merienda. Budbud Kabog is often paired with hot chocolate (sikwate) or ripe mango, making it one of the signature native treats of the area.
