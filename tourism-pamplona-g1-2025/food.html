Food & Accommodations

Taste and stay in comfort

Local Delicacies

Budbud Kabog

A well-loved native delicacy made from millet grain, wrapped in banana leaves. Soft, sweet, and often paired with hot chocolate or brewed coffee. It is one of the specialties that can be found in local markets and roadside vendors around Pamplona.

Inato Chicken

Grilled native chicken marinated with simple yet flavorful spices. Served in many local eateries including Raf and Nat’s Kan-anan. A top choice for affordable and satisfying meals.

Native Chicken Tinola

A warm, ginger-based soup made from free-range native chicken. Popular in Pamplona’s colder upland sitios where the climate enhances its comforting taste. Perfect for relaxing after a long day of travel.

Suman

A classic sticky rice delicacy cooked in coconut milk and wrapped in banana leaves. Commonly enjoyed during early mornings or local celebrations. Many households and market stalls prepare it fresh.

Torta

A small, round, flat cake that is a specialty in Negros Oriental. It is often made with eggs, flour, and sugar.

Fresh Backyard Produce

Pamplona is rich in vegetables grown from backyard and upland farms. Squash, eggplant, okra, malunggay, and other organic ingredients are commonly used in everyday dishes. Locals enjoy fresh, homegrown meals year-round.

Popular Place to Eat

Raf and Nat’s Kan-anant

A popular spot known for serving homestyle Filipino dishes cooked fresh daily. Favorites include tinola, kaldereta, adobo, inato chicken, and other lutong-bahay meals. Cuisine: Filipino, Lutong-Bahay.

Cuisine: Filipino, Lutong-Bahay

Café Alicia

A cozy highland café offering pastas, coffee, frappes, pizzas, and homemade cakes. Loved for its cool weather, mountain views, and relaxing atmosphere.

Cuisine: Café, Pasta, Drinks, Pastries

Local Carinderias & Market Stalls

Throughout Pamplona are small eateries serving freshly cooked rice meals, soups, grilled dishes, and native treats. Ideal for travelers looking for cheap and authentic Filipino food. Cuisine: Filipino, Native Delicacies

Cuisine: Filipino, Lutong-Bahay, Native Delicacies

Sakada Cafe Farm

A local restaurant famous for grilled meats, fresh vegetables, and traditional Filipino dishes. Offers a farm-to-table feel with flavors reflecting Pamplona’s upland produce. Great for families and visitors wanting hearty meals at affordable prices.

Cuisine:Filipino, Grilled, Farm-to-Table

Accommodation Tips

  • Book in advance during fiestas, weekends, and holiday seasons.
  • Highland stays offer cooler temperatures and scenic views.
  • Some homestays offer meals using fresh produce from their own farms.
  • Transport is easy through tricycles and habal-habal from the town center.
  • Locals are friendly and willing to help visitors with directions and recommendations.
