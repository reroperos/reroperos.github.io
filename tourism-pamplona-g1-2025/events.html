Celebrate culture and tradition in Dumaguete

Yamog Festival

The Premier Festival of Pamplona

When: October 12th

The Yamog Festival is a yearly celebration in the municipality of Pamplona, Negros Oriental that honors the local harvest, particularly fruits, vegetables, and coffee. "Yamog," meaning "dew" in the local dialect, inspires a festival theme of waking up to a dewy, abundant landscape. The event features street dancing, cultural performances, a beauty pageant, and is a featured part of the larger Buglasan Festival of Festivals.

Festival Highlights:
  • Street Dancing Competition
  • Grand Showdown
  • Festival Queen Pageant & Costume
  • Agricultural Theme Booths
  • Buglasan Festival Participation

Annual Events Calendar

Nuestra Señora del Pilar

October 12th

Pamplona, Negros Oriental celebrates its patron saint, Nuestra Señora del Pilar (Our Lady of the Pillar), every October 12th. The town’s annual fiesta is one of its most important events, bringing together residents and visitors for a week filled with unity, joy, and devotion. The celebration features a vibrant mix of activities such as colorful parades, lively street dancing, food fairs, sports tournaments, and nightly live bands. As the community honors its patron saint, the festival becomes both a religious observance and a cultural showcase that highlights the spirit and tradition of Pamplona.

Kasulad Festival

October 1-12

The Kasulad Festival is an annual celebration in Pamplona, Negros Oriental, held on October 12 to celebrate the town fiesta. The celebration has been known to include events like the Kasulad Festival and also marks the feast day for its patron saint, Nuestra Señora del Pilar. In 2005, the town also launched the Kasulad Festival, which ran from October 1-12

Sugar Rush x Montefrio Uphill Run

December 20-21

The Sugar Rush x Montefrio Uphill Run is a challenging outdoor race in Pamplona, Negros Oriental that takes runners through scenic mountain roads and cool highland landscapes. It highlights the town’s natural beauty while giving participants an exciting uphill adventure. The event attracts both locals and visitors who enjoy fitness, nature, and fresh mountain air.
